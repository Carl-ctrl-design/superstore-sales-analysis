# Superstore Retail Sales Performance Analysis

**Tools:** Microsoft Excel | PivotTables | Power Query | KPI Dashboard  
**Dataset:** Kaggle Superstore Sales Dataset (9,994 rows, 2014–2017)  
**Author:** Carlton Waiti  

## Business Problem
A retail chain wanted to understand what was driving revenue and margin performance across regions, product categories, and customer segments — and where the business was losing money despite high sales volumes.

## What I Analyzed
- Revenue and profit by product category and sub-category
- Regional performance comparison by profit margin
- Impact of discount rates on profitability
- Customer segment value analysis (Consumer vs Corporate vs Home Office)
- Year-over-year revenue trends (2014–2017)

## Key Findings
1. Tables and Bookcases generate high revenue but negative profit margins — the business is losing money on these sub-categories
2. Discounts above 20% consistently produce loss-making orders — 1,458 orders in the dataset are loss-making due to excessive discounts
3. The Central region has the lowest profit margin (7.9%) despite being the third largest by revenue
4. Technology is the highest-revenue AND highest-profit category
5. No-discount orders average a 29.5% profit margin vs -119% at the highest discount band

## Recommendations
1. Implement a discount approval policy — cap standard discounts at 20%
2. Review Tables and Bookcases supplier contracts or consider discontinuing low-margin SKUs
3. Investigate Central region operations — high discount rate (24%) suggests a pricing strategy problem
4. Prioritize Corporate segment growth — highest margin per segment
